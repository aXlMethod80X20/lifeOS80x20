Module 01 — Bits

The Latticework

Technology moves exponentially. Your mental operating system must keep pace. Build a latticework of models across disciplines — then use them to cut through noise, find deep focus, and make decisions with clarity.

01

Inversion

Instead of asking how to succeed, ask how you would certainly fail — then avoid those things.

Munger's favorite tool. Don't ask "how do I build a great product?" Ask "what would make this product terrible?" Invert the problem. Avoid stupidity rather than seeking brilliance. In a world flooded with information, knowing what to ignore is the real edge.

02

Second-Order Thinking

First-level thinking is fast and obvious. Second-level thinking asks: and then what?

Howard Marks: everyone sees the obvious move. The edge is in seeing the consequences of the consequences. In tech, first-order thinking ships the feature. Second-order thinking asks what behavior it incentivizes, what breaks at scale, what competitors do in response.

03

Circle of Competence

Know the boundaries of what you truly understand. Operate inside. Expand deliberately.

The size of your circle matters less than knowing its edge. In a fast-moving software world, your competence surface area shrinks daily unless you deliberately maintain it. Deep focus work — not shallow content consumption — is how you expand the circle.

04

Margin of Safety

Build redundancy into every system. Never operate at the edge of capacity.

Engineers call it fault tolerance. Investors call it margin of safety. Sleep, recovery, financial runway, code test coverage — the buffer you maintain when things are good is what saves you when things go wrong. Fragile systems optimize for efficiency. Antifragile systems optimize for survival.

05

Asymmetric Risk/Reward

Seek bets where the downside is capped but the upside is uncapped. This is convexity.

The core thesis of this entire system. In career, investment, and skill-building — structure your exposure so that you survive the worst case and benefit disproportionately from the best case. Small, repeated, low-cost bets with open-ended upside. That's the convex operator's edge.

06

Deep Focus Protocol

Distraction is the tax on shallow thinking. Depth is the moat in an age of noise.

Block 2-4 hour windows. Kill notifications. Single-task. The ability to sustain deep concentration on cognitively demanding work is becoming rare and therefore increasingly valuable. Protect this capacity like your most critical asset — because it is.

"You've got to have models in your head. And you've got to array your experience — both vicarious and direct — on this latticework of models."
— Charlie Munger

Module 03 — Capital

The Barbell Protocol

Taleb's antifragile portfolio: be hyper-conservative with most of your capital, and hyper-aggressive with a small portion. Nothing in the middle. The middle is where fragility hides.

80% Safe / Linear
20% Convex

Linear Investments

Predictable returns, capped upside

These are the foundation. Boring by design. They preserve capital, generate steady compounding, and let you sleep at night. The 80% exists so the 20% can take real risk.

  • Broad index funds (S&P 500, total market)
  • Government & investment-grade bonds
  • High-yield savings / money market
  • Dollar-cost averaging — always

Convex Investments

Capped downside, uncapped upside

This is where asymmetry lives. You can lose your stake but never more. The upside, however, has no ceiling. Structure small bets that benefit from volatility and disorder.

  • Asymmetric skill acquisition (coding, AI)
  • Early-stage / angel investments
  • Options strategies (defined risk)
  • Building equity in your own projects

Markowitz & the Efficient Frontier

Harry Markowitz showed that diversification isn't just about spreading bets — it's about finding the portfolio mix that maximizes return for a given level of risk. The efficient frontier is the set of optimal portfolios. Anything below it means you're taking more risk than necessary.

The barbell protocol positions you at both ends of the frontier: maximum safety (bonds, index) and maximum convexity (asymmetric bets). By avoiding the mushy middle, you get a portfolio that is antifragile — it actually benefits from volatility and disorder.

"The barbell strategy remedies the problem that risks of rare events are incomputable and fragile to estimation error."
— Nassim Nicholas Taleb, Antifragile
